Tablet Information
PARACETAMOL

The basic uses of paracetamol include:
Pain Relief: Effective for mild to moderate pain, such as headaches, toothaches, and muscle aches.
Fever Reduction: Used to lower fever in adults and children.
Paracetamol is widely regarded for its safety when used as directed, making it a common choice for managing everyday aches and pains.

Side Effects

While paracetamol is generally safe when taken as directed, it can have side effects, especially if taken in excessive doses. Some possible side effects include:
Liver Damage: Overdose can lead to severe liver injury, which can be life-threatening.
Allergic Reactions: Rarely, it may cause allergic reactions, including skin rashes, swelling, or difficulty breathing.
Gastrointestinal Issues: Some people may experience nausea or upset stomach.
Kidney Damage: Long-term use in high doses may affect kidney function.
It's important to follow dosage guidelines and consult a healthcare professional if you have any concerns.

What is its PURPOSE

Paracetamol, also known as acetaminophen, is a commonly used medication primarily for relieving pain and reducing fever. It works by inhibiting certain chemicals in the brain that signal pain and temperature. Paracetamol is effective for mild to moderate pain, such as headaches, toothaches, muscle aches, and menstrual cramps, and it is often recommended as a safer alternative to nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) for certain populations, including children and those with specific health conditions.
